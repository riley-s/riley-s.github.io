Mental Health, Covid-19 and Natural Language Processing

A Twitter data visualisation project

On March 11, 2020, the World Health Organisation declared Covid-19 a global pandemic. Consequently, many countries, including Australia, enacted lockdowns, curfews, and social distancing measures to prevent the spread of the disease. While these restrictive measures may have worked in the short-term for public health, it is unknown whether these measures has caused harm to our mental health. Using Twitter as a data source, this project gathered tweet across Australia's biggest cities (>100,000 population) an ran them through the BERT and DistilBERT NLP model. The senitment analysis indicates the discussion on mental health and Covid-19 are mainly negative in natural. Changing the sentiment surrounding these two topics, mental health and Covid-19, will help to improve the lives of individuals suffering with a mental illness.
